Material Study · Art Direction

fractal glass

Light bent through faceted, ribbed and prismatic glass. A refraction language that slots straight into the iridescent / liquid-chrome moodboard — carried on poster moments, kept quiet on functional UI.
What it is

Fractal glass simulates looking at an image through faceted or crystalline glass — producing kaleidoscopic refractions and prism-like distortions. The interplay of soft gradients and sharp refractions reads as futuristic yet dreamlike: a glitch in the system crossed with a hazy late-night cityscape.

Its natural homes are poster design, album covers and experimental branding — the exact surfaces where our label wants to feel like an object, not an app. We treat it as a refraction lens laid over the plum backdrop and the neon spectrum, never as a new color.

Also called
Reeded / fluted glass — vertical ribbed lensing
Prismatic glass — light split into hues
Faceted / crystal — shattered refraction
Frosted / glassmorphism — soft distortion
All five below are built live in CSS + SVG — no image assets.

03 — Recipe · the building blocks
01 · Ribs
repeating-linear-gradient(90deg,
  #fff2 0, #0003 6px, #fff2 12px)
Alternating light/shadow bands read as cylindrical glass reeds.
02 · Blur
backdrop-filter:
  blur(6px) saturate(1.2)
Softens whatever sits behind so the ribs feel like real glass.
03 · Warp
feTurbulence +
  feDisplacementMap
  → filter:url(#fg)
SVG fractal noise displaces pixels for true prismatic distortion.
Rule of restraint — refraction rides the plum & neon palette; it never introduces a new hue. Poster tier gets the full warp; UI tier gets ribs + blur only.
